    <div>
      <div className="font-semibold text-white">Supabase (Backend & Storage)</div>

      <div className="mt-2">
        <strong className="text-white">Do they store face data?</strong> Yes. Supabase temporarily stores:
        uploaded photos (which may contain faces) in Supabase Storage under our account.
      </div>

      <div className="mt-2">
        <strong className="text-white">Why do they store it?</strong> We upload photos to create a short-lived signed
        URL that our backend uses to securely retrieve the image during processing. This avoids sending
        large image files inside the analysis request payload and improves reliability (e.g., if the
        app is backgrounded or a retry is needed).
      </div>

      <div className="mt-2">
        <strong className="text-white">How long is it stored?</strong>
        <ul className="mt-2 list-disc space-y-1 pl-5">
          <li>
            <strong className="text-white">Selfies:</strong> We delete uploaded photos after the analysis attempt
            completes. If deletion fails due to network or unexpected errors, we run automated cleanup to
            remove any remaining photo uploads within <strong>6 hours</strong>.
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div className="mt-2">
        <strong className="text-white">Why these lengths?</strong> The <strong>6-hour</strong> window
        exist only as short reliability fallback (e.g., client disconnects, network errors, or retries) while
        minimizing retention of face photos.
      </div>
    </div>

    <div>
      <div className="font-semibold text-white">PostHog (Analytics)</div>

      <div className="mt-2">
        <strong className="text-white">Do they store face data?</strong> No. We do not send uploaded photos to PostHog.
      </div>

      <div className="mt-2">
